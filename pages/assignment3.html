Aglio e Olio

One of the simplest recipes I know that never disappoints: Aglio e Olio.

This is easily one of my go-tos when I know I have pasta and I need to whip up something fast! It's perfect with grilled herb-rubbed chicken, meatballs or shrimp! This recipe will include a few of my personal favorite ingredients, though it can really be tailored to however you like your pasta (which can be spaghetti, linguine, or even shorter ones like penne)!

One thing to keep in mind, with a dish as simple as this, fresh and high quality ingredients shine. If you can, try using extra virgin olive oil and fresh herbs, as well as parmesan blocks rather than packaged, grated cheese.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb spaghetti
  • 1 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 6-8 garlic cloves
  • 3 tablespoons olives
  • cherry tomatoes (to your liking)
  • 1/2 cup of shredded parmesan
  • 2 teaspoons chili flakes
  • 2 teaspoons fresh basil and parsley
  • salt and pepper (to your liking)

Instructions

  1. Chop the garlic into thin slices (do NOT mince). If you do not have cherry tomatoes, you can dice 1/4 of a normal tomato.
  2. Start boiling the spaghetti as instructed by the box. Add a little olive oil and salt while it's boiling if you would like.
  3. In another pan, add the olive oil and start the heat at medium. Add the sliced garlic and wait for it to simmer. The instant it simmers, bring the heat down to low-medium.
  4. Allow the garlic to simmer for around 8 minutes, or until it appears golden, stirring continuously. When it reaches a gold color, turn the heat off and add a ladle of pasta water to the oil. This ensures the garlic stops cooking so it doesn't burn. It can be tricky getting the timing correct, so stay with it as it cooks!
  5. By now, the pasta and garlic-olive-oil should be done. Combine the two in a bowl and toss. Add the salt and pepper, chili flakes, herbs, olives, tomatoes, and shredded parmesan, and continue tossing.
  6. Serve with grilled chicken, shrimp, meatballs, bread, or enjoy plain. It can be eaten hot or cooled in the fridge. Buon Appetito!
